Lesson 5 - Direct Proportion

ENGAGE

Nicole is trying to budget her use of cell phone load’ so she determined how much she is spending each time she makes a call. The table below shows her observations.

Minutes of Cells Cellphone load Spent
1 ₱6.00
2 ₱12.00
3 ₱18.00
4 ₱24.00
5 ₱30.00

If she is making an average of 30 minutes of calls every week, how much cell phone load is she spending for that?

EXPLORE

Notice how the amount of cell phone load spent increases as the number of minutes of calls increases. Based on what you have learned from the previous lesson, we can solve this problem using proportion.

1 minute: ₱6.00=30 minutes :n

1xn=6x30
1n=180
1n/1=180/1
n=180		 Check
1/8=30/180
1x180=?30x6
180=/180

Nicole spends P180.00 cell phone load for her weekly calls.

EXPLAIN

The problem in the previous page shows a direct proportion, wherein as one quantity increases, the other quantity increases proportionally. Let us take a closer look:

Minutes of Cells Cellphone load Spent
1 ₱2250.00
2 ₱1800.00
3 ₱1350.00
4 ₱900.00
5 ₱450.00

Notice that as the number of minutes of calls increases, the cell phone load spent increases correspondingly.

The ratios of the number of minutes of calls made and the corresponding cell phone load spent are equal and proportional. Hence, this represents a direct proportion.

Direct Proportion

When two quantities are related such that an increase (or decrease) in the first quantity causes an increase (or decrease) in the second quantity proportionally then they exhibit direct proportion.

As mentioned earlier, the situation in the Engage part of this lesson represents a direct proportion since the cell phone load spent increases as the number of minutes of calls increases proportionally. Similarly, Table A represents a direct proportion because the salary deduction decreases as the number of absences decreases proportionally as well.

When two quantities are in direct proportion, we can say that they are directly proportional.
