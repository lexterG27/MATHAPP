Lesson 1 - Addition and Subtraction of Decimals

ENGAGE

Miss Cruz asked her two pupils to write on the board the proper way of reading the decimal number 35.008. The two pupils gave their answers as shown below.

Name of Pupil Answer
Rachel thirty-five and eight thousandths
Wally thirty-five eight thousandths

EXPLORE

To answer the question, let us use the place value chart below to represent the decimal number property.

Whole Numbers
Tens Ones Decimal Point
3 5 .

Decimal Part
Tenths Hundredths Thousandths
0 0 8

A decimal point separates the whole number and the decimal part of a decimal number. It is read as “and”. Thus, the decimal number 35.008 is read as “thirty-five and eight thousandths.”

Therefore, among the two pupils, Rachel gave the correct answer.

EXPLAIN

The word decimal comes from the Latin word decima, meaning tenth part. Although we are using different notation, we are still considering the nonnegative rational numbers.

In Chemistry, the atomic mass of hydrogen is 1.007984 amu. Use the place value chart to read and understand this decimal number.

Use the place value chart to find the value of each digit of the atomic mass.

Read: one and seven hundred ninety-four hundred thousandths
Standard Form: 1.00794
