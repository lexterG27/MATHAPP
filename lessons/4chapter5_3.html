Lesson 3 - Describing Triangles and Quadrilaterals

EXPLORE

Mr. Tabilang bought a cartolina for his Math class. He needs two triangles of the same size without wastring any part of the cartolina. How will he divide it ?

What shapes are formed ?

A triangle is a polygon with 3 sides and 3 angles. A polygon is closed figure made up of several line segments that are joined together. The line segments are called slides and do not cross each other. There are exactly two sides that meet at a point.

A quadrilateralis a polygon with 4 sides and 4 angles.
