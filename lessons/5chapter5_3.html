EXPLAIN

If the simplest form of two or more ratios are the same or equal, then the ratios are equivalent. To know if two or more ratios are equivalent, you can express them in simplest form.

Example 1 Tell whether the given ratios are equivalent.

15/60 and 5/20

For 15/60 the GCF is 15
15/60+15/15=1/4
The simplest form of 15/60 is 1/4		 For 5/20 the GCF is 5.
5/20+5/5=1/4
The simplest of 5/20 is 1/4

The simplest from are both equal to 1/4. Therefore the given ratios are equivalent.

Example 2

16/12 and 75/100

For 16/12 the GCF is 4
16/12+4/4=4/3
The simplest form of 16/12 is 4/3		 For 75/100 the GCF is 25.
75/100+25/25=3/4
The simplest of 75/100 is 1/4
