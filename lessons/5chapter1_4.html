Lesson 4 - Order of Operation on Whole Numbers

Engage

A teacher wrote the expression (4 + 2 x 5) ÷7 + 3 on the board and called two pupils solve it.
Below are the solutions of two pupils.

Whose solution do you think is correct?

EXPLORE

The expression can be solved by following the rules on order of operations, this is called the PMDAS rule which stands for parentheses, multiplication, division, addition, and subtraction.

In any numerical expression that has more than one operation perform the operation inside the parentheses first. Then perform multiplication and division working from left to right Lastly. Perform addition and subtraction working from left to right.

Hence, according to the rule. Pupil 2’s solution correct (4 + 2 x 5) ÷ 7 + 3
Solve the operations inside the parenthesis
= (4 + 10) ÷ 7 + 3 Multiply 2 and 5 first
= 14 ÷ 7 + 3 Add 4 and 10
= 2 + 3 Divide 14 by 7
= 5 Add 2 and 3

EXPLAIN

400 ÷ 2 x 5 – 120 + 5 Divide 400 by 2.
= 200 x 5 – 120 + 5 Multiply 200 by 5.
= 1000 – 120 + 5 Subtract 120 from 1000.
= 880 + 5 Then, add.
= 885

Note: we work from left to right when there are only addition and subtraction. We also work from left to right when there are only multiplication and division.
