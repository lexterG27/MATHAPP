Lesson 3 - Multiplying Fractions and Whole Numbers

ENGAGE

Robert has 3 gallons of paint. HE used 3/4 of the paint. How many gallons of paint did he use?

EXPLORE

One way to think about this problem is to rewrite the problem.
In other words, what is 3/4 of 3?
As a number sentence, 3/4 x 3 = ?
An illustration can help you solve this problem.

How many fourths are there in total?
There are nine-fourths in total. SO, 3/4 x 3 = 9/4.
Therefore, Robert used 9/4 gallons of paint or 2 1/4 gallons of paint.
