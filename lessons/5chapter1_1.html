In the place value chart, notice that the digit are grouped into sets of three digits. Each group of three digifts is called a period. The word 'units;, 'thousands', and 'millions' among others, are used to name periods in our numeration system. For example, what is the value of 9 in the standard form 92337853? What about in the numerical 12609803? Notice that the place value of 9 in the numerals 92337853 is ten millions. Its value is 90 000 000. EXPLAIN

Numbers are grouped into periods and are separated by spaces. Each period consist of three digits. Ones, tens, hundreds, thoudands , hundred thousands, millions and hundred millions are called place values. Place value referes to the position that a digit occupies in the numerals. The value of digits depends on its place in numeral. Further, the value of each place is ten times greater than the value of the place to its right. Consider the numeral 21 708 560.
